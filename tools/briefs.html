Don't order / limits

Don'tUnstable angina, acute MI symptoms, or high-risk acute chest pain in an outpatient pathway; route to emergency/inpatient care.
Don'tLow-risk screening or routine annual surveillance without symptom, risk change, abnormal prior test, or management-changing question.
Don'tSevere symptomatic aortic stenosis - stress testing of ANY modality is high-risk; assess and treat the AS first. Vasodilator stress (regadenoson/dipyridamole) is contraindicated, AND exercise testing is itself contraindicated in symptomatic severe AS, while dobutamine raises the LV outflow gradient and myocardial O2 demand - so there is no safe routine substitute. If ischemia evaluation is essential, use anatomic imaging (coronary CT/cath) or stress only in a monitored setting after AS assessment. Supervised exercise is acceptable only in ASYMPTOMATIC severe AS to unmask symptoms (ASNC 2016 SPECT MPI; ACC/AHA 2021 Chest Pain).
Don'tHigh-grade AV block (Mobitz II or 3rd-degree) without a functioning pacemaker, with vasodilator stress - contraindicated (ASNC 2016 SPECT MPI; FDA Lexiscan label).
Don'tActive bronchospasm or severe reactive airway disease with vasodilator stress - avoid; use exercise or dobutamine instead (ASNC 2016 SPECT MPI; FDA Lexiscan label).
Don'tRecent (<12mo) normal MPI: defer a repeat unless new symptoms, a new ischemic ECG finding, or a management-changing decision is documented (ACC/AHA 2021 Chest Pain).
Don'tMetabolic Dx (diabetes/hyperlipidemia) is never sufficient alone; pair it with a cardiac symptom or finding as the trigger (Noridian L34324 stress LCD).
Don'tDon't repeat MPI within 12 months in asymptomatic CAD; if a prior <12-month MPI was normal, defer unless new symptoms/ECG change (ACC/AHA 2021).
Don'tPre-op: order only before intermediate/high-risk surgery in an intermediate/high CAD-risk patient who can't perform an ETT and has no stress test within 1 yr. Don'tDon't: severe hypertension that can't be controlled prior to stress (e.g., resting >200/110) — control BP first, then test.
Don'tStress MPI is reserved for symptomatic intermediate/high-risk patients to answer an ischemia question; risk-factor-only patients with no symptoms fall outside that indication, so screening MPI does not establish a covered ischemia question.

Don'tSymptoms without an anatomic outflow obstruction confirmed by venography/IVUS.
Don'tDo not stent asymptomatic/incidental iliac compression or CEAP C1-C3 reflux-only disease; treat the superficial reflux instead (AVF/SVS 2020).

Don'tStenting is an outflow-obstruction pathway, not a superficial-reflux or cosmetic pathway. Don'tAnatomic iliac compression alone is common and is not, by itself, an indication to stent. Don'tBefore attributing lower-extremity swelling to venous outflow obstruction, non-vascular causes (CHF, renal, lymphatic, hypoalbuminemia, medication) must be excluded.
