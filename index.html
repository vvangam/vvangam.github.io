About Me

I’m a healthcare professional and health informatics specialist dedicated to transforming clinical insights into innovative technological solutions. I hold an MBBS from Gandhi Medical College & Hospital (May 2023) and a Master’s in Health Informatics from Boston University (Jan 2025), which have equipped me with a unique blend of hands-on patient care and data science expertise.

In my role as a Research Assistant at Boston University’s Department of Computer Science, I design and execute experiments on large medical datasets to evaluate and enhance machine learning models—even in the face of challenging conditions like label noise. My projects range from developing AI-driven diagnostic tools for Age-Related Macular Degeneration to integrating advanced machine learning techniques in ophthalmic imaging for diabetic retinopathy screening, underscoring my commitment to leveraging technology to improve diagnostic accuracy and foster equitable healthcare solutions.

Combining clinical experience from my time as a Doctor Intern at Gandhi Medical Hospital with advanced technical skills in Python, machine learning frameworks, and data analytics, I am passionate about merging evidence-based medicine with digital innovation. My goal is to continue driving healthcare advancements that not only enhance patient outcomes but also pave the way for smarter, more responsive health systems.
